Key Terms

Quick reference for the language used on your dashboard.
CM (Contribution Margin)
Revenue − COGS − Contribution Expenses (Sales/Account team salaries + super + commissions). The cash left after the team that produced the revenue is paid. Your CM bonus is calculated incrementally above the $1M baseline.
NP (Net Profit)
CM − OpEx (overheads not tied to projects). The bottom line. Your NP kicker pays in tiers above thresholds — every $100k more earns more.
LTM (Last Twelve Months)
Rolling sum of the last 4 complete quarters from Xero. Drives your projected commission and the maintenance bonus calculation.
YoY (Year-over-Year)
Same metric this quarter vs the same quarter last year. Shows whether you're growing the business compared to the previous period.
Maintenance Bonus
If the company stays profitable at $1M+ CM with no growth, you still earn a $10k baseline. This protects you from being penalised for steady-state years.
CM Baseline ($1M)
The floor below which CM bonus = $0. Above $1M, every dollar of CM earns at the band rate.
NP Kicker
Extra bonus paid when company NP crosses certain milestones ($100k / $300k / $700k / $1M / $1.3M / $1.5M). Tiered — bigger NP unlocks bigger bonuses.
Super-Inclusive
The dollar figures already include the 12% superannuation. So $46k commission means $41k to you + $5k super.
75% Quarterly / 25% Holdback
Each quarter pays 75% of commission earned to that point. The remaining 25% is held back and reconciled at year-end based on actual full-year CM/NP.
Quarterly True-Up
Each quarter we look at cumulative CM/NP, calculate the commission earned to date, subtract what's been paid in earlier quarters, and pay the difference (capped at 75% per quarter).
KPI Multiplier
A score from 0.7× to 1.3× applied to your variable comp. Currently locked at 1.0× — KPIs go live in a future pass.
Total Package
Base salary + super on base + CM commission + NP kicker. The full cost-to-MEC of employing you for the year.
