Key Terms

Quick reference for the language used on your dashboard.
Revenue (ex GST)
Money the company earns from your performances after stripping the 10% GST. This is what shows in Xero's P&L and what your commission is calculated on.
TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)
Your revenue total over the last 365 days. Updated each quarter — drives your quarterly commission. Smooths out seasonal variation so you don't have to wait until year-end to be paid.
Super-Inclusive
The dollar figures on your dashboard already include the 12% superannuation. So if it says $5,000 commission, that means $4,464 to you + $536 super = $5,000 total cost to MEC. You don't get extra super on top.
Realistic Range
The green zone on your Annual Performance Revenue slider — the $170k–$250k window where most of your year is likely to land. The slider moves slowly through this zone so you can dial in the exact figure easily.
Annual True-Up
Year-end catch-up payment. If your final TTM revenue would have earned you more than the sum of your quarterly payouts, the difference is paid as a top-up. See "What is the True-Up?" in the Overview tab for full details.
Quarterly Payout
Your commission split into 4 cash flows. Each quarter, we look at your TTM revenue and pay you 1/4 of the annual commission that level would earn — so cash flow stays steady through the year.
KPI Multiplier
A score from 0.7× to 1.3× applied to your variable comp. 50% achievement = 1.0×; 100% = 1.3×; 0% = 0.7×.
Base Salary
Your guaranteed annual salary, paid fortnightly, regardless of performance. Shown ex-super on your dashboard with super added separately.
Total Package
Base salary + super on base + commission earned + project bonuses. The full cost-to-MEC of employing you for the year.
$5k Kicker (38.75% band)
The boosted rate Nathan put in place to accelerate you past the $220k milestone. Higher than surrounding bands by design — beyond $220k, the rate returns to standard 10% incremental.
Effective Rate
The commission % you'd earn on the next dollar of revenue at your current position — base band rate × KPI multiplier.

What is the True-Up?

Your commission is calculated each quarter on your trailing 12-month revenue (TTM) and divided by 4. This means quarterly payouts are smoother and more predictable — you don't have to wait until year-end to get paid.

But there's a problem: if you have a slow start to the year and a big finish, the early quarterly payouts are calculated on lower TTM, so they pay less. By the time your TTM catches up at year-end, only the final quarter's payout reflects your strong year — and that's just 25% of the annual commission. You'd be underpaid.

The Annual True-Up fixes this. At year-end:

  • We compute what your full annual commission should be at your final Q4 TTM revenue.
  • We subtract everything we've already paid you across Q1–Q4.
  • The difference is paid as a year-end catch-up bonus.

This way, if you finish the year strong (e.g. you push past the $220k milestone in Q4), you get the full annual commission you earned — including the 38.75% kicker zone — not just 1/4 of it from the last quarter. Your base salary is never affected, and the true-up is never negative.

Example: You start the year with TTM at $150k (no commission below $195k). By Q4 you push TTM to $220k → annual entitlement ≈ $9.7k. If only Q4 paid out ($2.4k), the $7.3k true-up at year-end gets you to the full $9.7k.

How Your Variable Compensation Works

The thinking behind the plan, in plain English.

We pay you variable compensation because we want to reward exceptional performance. And we pay you when the customer pays — actual cash in the account means actual cash in your pocket.

That single sentence is the spine of the whole plan. Everything below follows from it. These are the principles we use to design how the bands, gates, and bonuses actually work — and the deal we’re making with you when you sign on.

The six principles

  1. Cash in is cash out. The day a customer’s cash lands at MEC is the day it counts toward your commission. Not the day a quote is sent. Not the day a booking is confirmed. The day the cash actually arrives. This is different to the old plan, and it does mean some commission will land later than it used to — especially for events booked months in advance. But it also means the cash you’re paid on is real, and the company isn’t out of pocket if a deal gets pulled.
  2. Same role, same plan. Every Sales Rep is on the same plan as every other Sales Rep. Every Performer is on the same plan as every other Performer. Where current rates differ from the standard shape today, that’s a transition position — we’ll bring everyone onto the same shape at the next plan refresh. Bespoke compensation belongs in your base salary, not in your commission curve.
  3. The harder you push, the more you earn per dollar. Bands climb. The first dollar of GP above your previous tier earns at a higher rate than the last dollar of the tier you just cleared. We want to reward stretch, not punish it. There’s no cap.
  4. Refunds reduce future commission. They never touch your base. If a gig is refunded or cancelled after you’ve been paid commission on it, that refund nets against your next commission cycle. No exceptions. But it never touches your base salary, and your paycheck never goes negative because of a refund.
  5. What lands while you’re employed is yours. If you ever leave MEC, any commission already paid out is yours. Any commission on cash that has already arrived at MEC, even if it hasn’t been paid out yet, is yours — in your final paycheck. Cash that arrives after your last day stays with the company.
  6. KPIs matter where your contract says they do. Some roles have KPIs spelled out in their employment agreement — if yours does, those KPIs are real. They will be tracked, reviewed quarterly, and they affect your eligibility for full variable pay. We will not put a KPI on you that we don’t intend to enforce. If a KPI feels wrong or out of date, raise it.

What we don’t pay variable comp on

Variable compensation is a sharp tool, and we use it for a narrow set of things. Plenty of behaviour we deeply value sits outside it:

  • Helping a colleague close a deal — noticed and recognised, but not split into your commission. That’s the team.
  • Doing your job exceptionally well in non-numerical ways — client experience, hand-offs, mentoring, the way you turn up. Reflected in your annual review and discretionary bonuses, not in the plan multiplier.
  • Effort in itself — we pay for what lands. Effort is necessary but not sufficient.

How the plan changes year to year

Expect a band tweak once a year — a small calibration based on what we’ve learned and where the business is heading. Bigger structural changes (new plan shape, addition of team-pool commissions if we go that way) will be communicated in advance and never applied retrospectively. Cash you’ve already earned is locked in.

— Nathan Op de Coul, Director, MEC Group
