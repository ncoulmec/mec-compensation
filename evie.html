Thresholds are placeholders for now: ≥30% = High Margin (green), ≥20% = Acceptable (amber), <20% = Low Margin (red). Company Guardrail

The company maintains a minimum margin floor to ensure variable compensation is funded by real profitability. If the rolling company margin drops below the guardrail threshold, variable compensation pauses — base salary is unaffected. This protects both you and the business during low-margin periods.

Quarterly True-Up Summary ?

Each quarter reconciles commission paid vs entitled. Differences flow through the running balance shown under each bar.

What is True-Up?

Your GP commissions are paid monthly so you get cash flow through the year instead of waiting until year-end. But monthly payouts are only an estimate — your real entitlement depends on your quarterly and annual GP.

At the end of each quarter we check:

  • Did you get paid more than you earned this quarter? The extra becomes a true-up balance — future commissions are reduced until the balance is worked off. Your base salary never moves.
  • Did you get paid less than you earned? Your month-3 commission tops up to match.

If a true-up balance is still sitting there at year-end, it carries into next year and nets against your first months' commissions. This way monthly payouts stay generous and fair, without anyone being over- or under-paid in the long run.

Example: you sell $70k of GP in month 1 and $0 in months 2-3. The monthly bands pay you about $4,100 in month 1. But at the end of the quarter, your entitlement on $70k quarterly is only $1,900. The $2,200 difference becomes your true-up balance — it'll offset what you earn in Q2 until worked off.
